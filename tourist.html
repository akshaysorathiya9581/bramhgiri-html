Brahmagiri is situated at a vantage point, from where it’s very easy to travel short distances and visit several local attractions. Of course, the resort is on the banks of the Kajali River, and it’s possible to walk through our nature trail and reach the river and experience its beauty. But, there are many other tourist attractions in the area that can be explored.

Ganpatipule

The Konkan area is famous for its clean and beautiful beaches. One such beach is adjacent to the small town of Ganapatipule. It is only 25 km from Ratnagiri and is about an hour’s drive from the Brahmagiri resort. The beach is a great tourist attraction and you can also see the ancient Swayambhu Ganapati Temple, right on the beach itself, which is a pilgrimage site.

MARLESHWAR WATERFALL

The famous waterfall is located in Marleshwar, which is part of the Ratnagiri district. The waterfall is adjacent to a cave temple, which is known to have sacred inscriptions carved within it. Every year, a pilgrimage to this temple takes place at the time of Makar Sankranti.

THIBA PALACE

In a bizarre episode of history, King Thiba, who rules Brahmadesha (Myanmar today) lived at a palace in the Ratnagiri district. After the British Imperial forces conquered Burma, King Thiba and his family were moved to Chennai. However, in 1910, the British built a massive Brahmi style palace for the King in Ratnagiri and moved him there to be kept under house arrest until his demise in 1919. The palace has become a tourist attraction in the area.
